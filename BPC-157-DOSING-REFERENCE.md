# BPC-157 Dosing Reference Guide for Researchers

BPC-157 (Body Protection Compound 157) is a synthetic pentadecapeptide consisting of 15 amino acids. This reference guide summarizes typical dosing protocols used in research settings.

## Standard Research Protocols

### Systemic / Subcutaneous
- **Low dose:** 1–2 mcg/kg body weight (~100–200 mcg for 100kg subject)
- **Standard dose:** 5–10 mcg/kg (~500 mcg–1 mg for 100kg subject)
- **Frequency:** Once or twice daily in most studies
- **Duration:** 2–4 weeks typical research cycle

### Oral Administration
- Higher doses used due to reduced bioavailability (typically 2–5x subcutaneous dose)
- Research suggests partial stability in gastric acid

### Topical / Localized
- Applied directly to area of interest in some wound-healing research

## Key Research Considerations

1. **Reconstitution:** Use bacteriostatic water (0.9% benzyl alcohol)
2. **Storage:** Lyophilized powder — store at -20°C; reconstituted — refrigerate at 2–8°C and use within 30 days
3. **COA Verification:** Always verify third-party lab testing before use in research
4. **Purity Standard:** Look for ≥98% purity via HPLC analysis
