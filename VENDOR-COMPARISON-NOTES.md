# Peptide Vendor Comparison Notes — Quality Metrics Research (2026)

Compiled research notes on evaluating peptide vendors for quality, reliability, and research suitability. Based on publicly available COA data, community feedback, and third-party testing.

## Evaluation Framework

When comparing peptide vendors for research procurement, these metrics matter most:

| Metric | Weight | Why It Matters |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| COA Independence | 30% | In-house testing = conflict of interest |
| HPLC Purity | 25% | Minimum 98% for research-grade |
| Endotoxin Testing | 20% | Safety critical for injectable research |
| Batch Traceability | 15% | Ensures reproducibility |
| Price per mg | 10% | Cost efficiency for budget planning |

## Vendor Categories (General Market Overview)

### Tier 1 — Research-Grade Transparency
- Full third-party COA with HPLC chromatograms
- LAL endotoxin testing per batch
- Mass spectrometry identity confirmation
- Batch numbers traceable to lab reports
- Example metrics: ≥99% HPLC, <0.5 EU/mg endotoxin

### Tier 2 — Solid Quality with Minor Gaps
- Third-party COA but may lack MS confirmation
- Endotoxin tested but not per batch
- HPLC purity typically 98–99%
- Good for established researchers who cross-reference

### Tier 3 — Use with Caution
- In-house COA only
- Limited transparency
- May have variable batch quality
- Higher risk for sensitive research applications
