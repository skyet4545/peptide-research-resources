## 🏪 Vendor Reviews

Choosing a reliable peptide vendor is one of the most critical decisions a researcher makes. Purity, sterility, and accurate labeling vary enormously across suppliers.

### Curated Review Sources

| Resource | What It Covers | Link |
|----------|----------------|------|
| **PeptideRadar Vendor Reviews** | In-depth reviews with CoA analysis, pricing, shipping | [peptideradar.net/reviews](https://peptideradar.net/reviews) |
| **PeptideRadar Best Companies List** | Ranked list of top vendors by purity scores | [peptideradar.net/best-peptide-companies](https://peptideradar.net/best-peptide-companies) |

### What to Look for in a Vendor

- **Third-party Certificate of Analysis (CoA):** Every batch should have HPLC purity ≥ 98%
- **Mass spectrometry verification:** Confirms molecular weight matches claimed peptide
- **Sterile manufacturing:** USP-grade water, endotoxin testing
- **Clear labeling:** Amino acid sequence, batch number, expiration date
- **Responsive support:** Will answer technical questions

### Red Flags

- ❌ No CoA available on request
- ❌ CoA from their own in-house lab (not third-party)
- ❌ Pricing significantly below market (often indicates lower purity)
- ❌ Vague product descriptions
- ❌ No HPLC data

### Reconstitution Reference

**Standard reconstitution:**
```
Volume (mL) = (Peptide amount in mg × 1000) / (Target concentration in mcg/mL)
```

Example: 5mg BPC-157, targeting 250mcg/mL → add 20mL bacteriostatic water

## ⚖️ Legal Resources

Peptide legality varies significantly by country and intended use. This section summarizes the regulatory landscape for common research peptides.

### Regulatory Status by Region

| Region | Research Use Status | Clinical Use Status |
|--------|--------------------|--------------------|
| United States | Legal for research (not human use) | Varies by peptide (some FDA-approved) |
| European Union | Varies by member state | Prescription-required for approved peptides |
| United Kingdom | Legal for research (not human use) | MHRA regulated |
| Australia | Restricted — TGA scheduled | Schedule 4 or 8 depending on peptide |
| Canada | Legal for research | Health Canada regulated |

### Key Regulatory Bodies

- **FDA (USA):** [fda.gov](https://www.fda.gov) — Center for Drug Evaluation and Research
- **EMA (EU):** [ema.europa.eu](https://www.ema.europa.eu) — European Medicines Agency
- **TGA (Australia):** [tga.gov.au](https://www.tga.gov.au) — Therapeutic Goods Administration
- **MHRA (UK):** [gov.uk/government/organisations/medicines-and-healthcare-products-regulatory-agency](https://www.gov.uk/government/organisations/medicines-and-healthcare-products-regulatory-agency)

### Important Notes

- Always verify current regulations in your jurisdiction
- "For research use only" labeling does not automatically grant legal protection for human use
- Import regulations may differ from domestic possession rules
- Some peptides (e.g., Tesamorelin/Egrifta) have FDA approval for specific clinical indications

### HPLC Purity Reading Guide

When reviewing a Certificate of Analysis:

1. **Purity %** — Look for ≥ 98% by HPLC (UV detection at 220nm)
2. **Single major peak** — Chromatogram should show one dominant peak
3. **Retention time** — Should be consistent batch-to-batch
4. **Mass confirmation** — ESI-MS or MALDI-TOF should match theoretical MW

## 📖 Glossary & Terminology

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **GHRH** | Growth Hormone-Releasing Hormone — stimulates pituitary GH release |
| **GH Secretagogue** | Any compound that stimulates GH release (e.g., Ipamorelin, GHRP-6) |
| **HPLC** | High-Performance Liquid Chromatography — gold standard for purity testing |
| **CoA** | Certificate of Analysis — batch-specific lab report |
| **Lyophilized** | Freeze-dried — standard storage form for research peptides |
| **Bacteriostatic Water** | Sterile water with 0.9% benzyl alcohol, used for reconstitution |
| **Half-life** | Time for plasma concentration to decrease by 50% |
| **Subcutaneous (SQ)** | Under the skin injection method |
| **Intramuscular (IM)** | Into muscle injection method |
| **Peptide Bond** | Covalent bond between amino acid residues |

> For a full glossary, visit [PeptideRadar.net](https://peptideradar.net)
