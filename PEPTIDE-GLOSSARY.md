# Complete Peptide Research Glossary

A comprehensive reference of terms used in peptide research. For the full interactive glossary, visit:
🔬 **https://peptideradar.net/glossary/index.html**

## A

**Acylation** — Chemical modification that adds an acyl group to increase lipophilicity and extend half-life (e.g., in semaglutide, liraglutide)

**Amino Acid** — The building blocks of peptides and proteins. 20 standard amino acids exist in biological systems.

**Analog** — A modified version of a natural peptide with altered structure for improved stability, potency, or selectivity.

**AOD-9604** — A modified fragment of human growth hormone (hGH176-191) studied for fat metabolism properties.

## B

**Bacteriostatic Water (BW)** — Sterile water containing 0.9% benzyl alcohol; standard reconstitution medium for multi-dose peptide vials.

**Bioavailability** — The fraction of administered compound that reaches systemic circulation unchanged.

**BPC-157** — Body Protection Compound 157; a synthetic pentadecapeptide derived from human gastric juice protein, studied for healing properties. Full guide: https://peptideradar.net/articles/bpc-157-complete-guide.html

**BDNF** — Brain-Derived Neurotrophic Factor; a protein that supports neuronal survival and growth. Some peptides (Semax) are studied for BDNF modulation.

## C

**Certificate of Analysis (COA)** — A document from an analytical laboratory verifying a product's identity, purity, and quality. For interpretation guide, see: https://peptideradar.net/blog/how-to-verify-peptide-purity.html

**CJC-1295** — A GHRH analog with extended half-life (via DAC modification). Studied for GH pulse amplification.

**COA Transparency** — The practice of vendors making independent, third-party COAs publicly available and batch-traceable.

## D–E

**DAC (Drug Affinity Complex)** — A technology that extends the half-life of peptides by binding to albumin (e.g., CJC-1295 DAC vs. CJC-1295 without DAC).

**Dipeptide** — A peptide containing exactly two amino acid residues.

**Endotoxin** — Lipopolysaccharides from bacterial cell walls; contaminants in injectable solutions that cause fever and inflammation. Tested via LAL assay.

**Epitalon** — A tetrapeptide (Ala-Glu-Asp-Gly) studied for telomerase activation and anti-aging properties. Full guide: https://peptideradar.net/blog/epitalon-guide.html

**ESI-MS** — Electrospray Ionization Mass Spectrometry; used to confirm peptide molecular weight and identity.

## F–G

**Fragment Analogs** — Peptides derived from specific fragments of larger proteins (e.g., AOD-9604 from HGH).

**GHK-Cu** — Glycine-Histidine-Lysine copper complex; a naturally occurring tripeptide studied for wound healing, anti-aging, and collagen synthesis. Full guide: https://peptideradar.net/articles/ghk-cu-peptide-guide.html

**GHRH** — Growth Hormone-Releasing Hormone; a peptide secreted by the hypothalamus that stimulates anterior pituitary GH release.

**GHRP** — Growth Hormone-Releasing Peptide; class of synthetic peptides that stimulate GH release (includes GHRP-6, Hexarelin).

**GLP-1** — Glucagon-Like Peptide-1; an incretin hormone. Synthetic analogs (semaglutide, tirzepatide) are used for diabetes/obesity.

## H–I

**Half-life (t½)** — The time required for plasma concentration to decrease by half. Lyophilized peptides in solution have different stability half-lives than in vivo plasma half-lives.

**HPLC** — High-Performance Liquid Chromatography; the gold standard analytical method for peptide purity testing. Purity ≥98% = research grade.

**Ipamorelin** — A selective GH secretagogue/GHRP that stimulates GH release without significant cortisol or prolactin elevation. Full guide: https://peptideradar.net/blog/ipamorelin-complete-guide.html

**IGF-1** — Insulin-Like Growth Factor 1; mediates many of GH's anabolic effects. Synthetic analog IGF-1 LR3 has extended half-life.

## L–M

**LAL Test** — Limulus Amebocyte Lysate test; standard assay for detecting bacterial endotoxins. <1 EU/mg target for research peptides.

**Lyophilization** — Freeze-drying process that removes water while preserving molecular structure; standard preparation for research peptides.

**Mass Spectrometry (MS)** — Analytical technique that identifies compounds by their mass-to-charge ratio; used for peptide identity confirmation.

**MK-677 (Ibutamoren)** — A non-peptide GH secretagogue (orally bioavailable); often grouped with peptides due to similar mechanisms.

## N–P

**NAP (Name/Address/Phone)** — Local business citation format important for local SEO.

**Peptide Bond** — The covalent bond (–CO–NH–) formed between amino acid residues in a peptide chain.

**Peptide Purity** — The percentage of the intended compound in a sample; ≥98% HPLC purity is the research-grade standard.

**PT-141 (Bremelanotide)** — A melanocortin receptor agonist studied for sexual function. Full guide: https://peptideradar.net/blog/pt-141-guide.html

## R–S

**Reconstitution** — The process of adding bacteriostatic or sterile water to a lyophilized peptide to create a solution.

**Selank** — A synthetic heptapeptide analog of tuftsin studied for anxiolytic and cognitive effects. Full guide: https://peptideradar.net/blog/selank-guide.html

**Semaglutide** — A long-acting GLP-1 receptor agonist. Full guide: https://peptideradar.net/blog/semaglutide-complete-guide.html

**Semax** — A heptapeptide analog of ACTH studied for cognitive enhancement and neuroprotection. Full guide: https://peptideradar.net/blog/semax-guide.html

**Solid-Phase Peptide Synthesis (SPPS)** — The predominant method for manufacturing synthetic peptides for research.

## T–Z

**TB-500** — Thymosin Beta-4 fragment; studied for systemic healing and tissue repair. Full guide: https://peptideradar.net/blog/tb-500-complete-guide.html

**Tirzepatide** — A dual GIP/GLP-1 receptor agonist. Full guide: https://peptideradar.net/blog/tirzepatide-complete-guide.html

**Thymalin** — A thymic peptide studied for immune system modulation and anti-aging.
